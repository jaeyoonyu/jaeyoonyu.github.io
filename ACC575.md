## Core Principles for Accounting Analytics

Beyond technical proficiency, successful analysts must develop a disciplined approach to data integrity. In this course, we emphasize:

* **Establishing Proper Expectations:** It is critical to establish a "skepticism-first" mindset in advance. Students must understand that structured data is not a finished product; assuming data is perfect leads to flawed conclusions.
* **Capabilities to Plan and Inspect:** Students must develop the capability to proactively plan for data anomalies and the technical skill to inspect issues by tracing "structured" results back to their original human-readable sources.

* **An Example Case Study**: When "Machine-Readable" Isn't "Truth". A prime example of why these principles matter is found in the article: [When Structured Data Isnâ€™t What the Company Reported](https://directedgar.blog/2026/01/28/when-structured-data-isnt-what-the-company-reported/). This case highlights a systematic software bug in Inline XBRL filings that inflated reported public float values by a factor of one million. While the data was technically "valid" to a computer, it was economically impossible. This reinforces the lesson that automation does not replace the need for professional judgment and source verification.
